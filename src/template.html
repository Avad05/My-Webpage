Bhootni Arc
open_in_new

In this classic horror-comedy storyline, the peaceful Gokuldham Society is rattled by sightings of a ghost. The arc begins around episode 413 and runs through approximately episode 425, where the residents—and especially Champaklal—encounter eerie, supernatural occurrences that have everyone spooked

This lighthearted storyline showcases a comical wedding for young Tapu in a dreamy, imaginative setting. It's not part of the main timeline but rather a fun, fantasy sequence from his childhood days.

Chori in Gokuldham

The "Chor in society" arc—where a 'thief' appears in the society—is episode 162. In that storyline, the Tapu Sena concocts a tale involving “khujli powder” (itching powder), and it gets linked to the idea of a ‘chor’ in the society

Gulabo in Society
open_in_new

Gulabo (also known as Gulabdi), portrayed by Simple Kaul, appears in Gokuldham and claims to be Jethalal’s wife—based on a marriage scene they once shot during his bachelorhood trip to Kashmir. The society gets embroiled in the drama, and things escalate to a court case.

Jhethalal in Pakistan
open_in_new

During a camel ride in Kutch, Jethalal unintentionally crosses into Pakistan and is apprehended by authorities who suspect him to be a spy. He ends up in a Pakistani jail, where, despite the initial hostility, he forms bonds with fellow inmates and even repairs a Pakistani officer's phone using his electronic skills

Babita and Iyer Divorce
open_in_new

The arc kicks off when Iyer overhears Babita talking to her friend Maushami, where she expresses unhappiness in their marriage and says she doesn’t truly love him—comments that were meant to comfort her friend, not reflect her real feelings. Iyer misunderstands this and is heartbroken.
